Paneer Butter Masala

“Rich, creamy & totally comforting”

Serves: 3-4 people

Prep Time: 15 minutes

Marination Time: 30 minutes

Cooking Time: 25 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 250g paneer (cubed)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 medium onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves)
  • 1/4 cup fresh cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil and butter in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Stir in ginger-garlic paste and cook until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add tomato puree and cook until the mixture thickens and the oil starts to separate.
  5. Mix in turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Add paneer cubes and gently mix to coat them in the masala.
  7. Sprinkle garam masala and kasuri methi. Stir well.
  8. Pour in fresh cream and simmer for 5 minutes on low heat.
  9. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  10. Serve hot with naan, roti, or jeera rice.

FAQs:

Q: Can I use frozen paneer?
A: Yes, just thaw it properly and soak in warm water for 10–15 minutes to soften before cooking.

Q: What can I use instead of fresh cream?
A: You can substitute with full-fat milk or cashew paste for a creamy texture.

Q: Is Paneer Butter Masala spicy?
A: It’s mildly spiced. You can adjust the chili powder and garam masala to suit your taste.

Q: How do I store leftovers?
A: Keep in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 days. Reheat gently before serving.
