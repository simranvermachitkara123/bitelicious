Uttapam

“Thick, Soft, and Topped with Fresh Veggies – Delicious South Indian Uttapam.”

Serves: 3-4 people

Prep Time: 15 minutes

Rest Time: 8 hours (fermentation)

Cooking Time: 15-20 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 2 cups dosa rice (parboiled rice)
  • 1/2 tsp fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • Oil for cooking
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 small tomato (finely chopped)
  • 1 small carrot (grated)
  • 2 green chilies (finely chopped, optional)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (chopped)

Instructions:

  1. Wash and soak urad dal with fenugreek seeds for 4-6 hours.
  2. Wash and soak dosa rice separately for 4-6 hours.
  3. Grind urad dal to a smooth and fluffy batter using water gradually.
  4. Grind rice coarsely and mix with the urad dal batter.
  5. Add salt and mix well. Cover and leave for fermentation 8 hours or overnight.
  6. Heat a non-stick pan or griddle and lightly grease with oil.
  7. Pour a ladle of batter and spread slightly thick.
  8. Sprinkle chopped onions, tomatoes, carrot, green chilies, and coriander on top.
  9. Cook on medium heat until the base is golden and the vegetables slightly soften. Flip if desired, or cook only one side for traditional Uttapam.
  10. Serve hot with coconut chutney or sambar.

FAQs:

Q: Can I make Uttapam with leftover dosa batter?
A: Yes, Uttapam can be made using the same fermented batter as dosa or idli.

Q: How do I make Uttapam fluffy?
A: Use well-fermented batter and spread it slightly thick on the pan. Avoid pressing down too much.

Q: Can I add other vegetables?
A: Absolutely! Capsicum, onions, carrots, tomatoes, or even grated zucchini work well.

Q: Can I store Uttapam batter?
A: Store in the fridge for up to 2 days. Stir gently before making Uttapam.
