Serves: 3-4 people

Prep Time: 10 minutes

Rest Time: 8 hours (fermentation)

Cooking Time: 15-20 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup raw rice (sona masoori or parboiled rice)
  • 1/4 cup cooked rice
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut or coconut milk
  • 1/4 tsp active dry yeast (optional, for faster fermentation)
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for greasing pan

Instructions:

  1. Wash and soak raw rice for 4-6 hours.
  2. Grind soaked rice with grated coconut (or coconut milk) and cooked rice to a smooth batter.
  3. Add sugar, salt, and yeast (if using) and mix well.
  4. Cover and allow the batter to ferment overnight or for 8 hours until it rises and becomes slightly bubbly.
  5. Heat a non-stick or appam pan. Lightly grease with oil.
  6. Pour a ladle of batter in the center and swirl the pan to spread the edges thinly while keeping the center soft.
  7. Cover and cook for 2-3 minutes until edges are crisp and the center is cooked.
  8. Serve hot with coconut milk, vegetable stew, or chutney.

FAQs:

Q: Can I skip yeast?
A: Yes, fermentation naturally occurs with cooked rice and time. Yeast just speeds up the process.

Q: How do I get crispy edges?
A: Swirl the batter thinly on a hot pan and cook uncovered for crispiness.

Q: Can I use coconut milk instead of grated coconut?
A: Yes, coconut milk works well for a softer, spongy center.

Q: How to store leftover batter?
A: Refrigerate for 1-2 days. Stir gently before making appams.
