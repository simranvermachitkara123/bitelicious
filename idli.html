Idli

“Soft, Fluffy, and Steamed to Perfection – Classic South Indian Idli.”

Serves: 4-5 people

Prep Time: 10 minutes

Rest Time: 8 hours (fermentation)

Cooking Time: 15 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 2 cups idli rice (parboiled rice)
  • 1/2 tsp fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed

Instructions:

  1. Wash and soak urad dal with fenugreek seeds for 4-6 hours.
  2. Wash and soak idli rice separately for 4-6 hours.
  3. Grind urad dal to a smooth and fluffy batter using water gradually.
  4. Grind rice coarsely and mix with the urad dal batter.
  5. Add salt and mix well. Cover and leave for fermentation 8 hours or overnight.
  6. Grease the idli molds and pour the fermented batter into them.
  7. Steam in an idli steamer or pressure cooker (without whistle) for 12-15 minutes.
  8. Let them cool slightly, remove from molds, and serve hot with chutney or sambar.

FAQs:

Q: Can I use instant rice for idli?
A: Yes, but traditional idli rice gives better texture and fluffiness.

Q: How do I make idlis soft?
A: Ensure proper fermentation, grind dal to a fluffy batter, and do not overcook.

Q: Can I make vegan idli?
A: Idli is naturally vegan since it contains no dairy or eggs.

Q: Can I store leftover batter?
A: Store in the fridge for up to 2 days. Stir gently before using for steaming.
