Medu Vada

“Crispy on the Outside, Soft on the Inside – Classic South Indian Medu Vada.”

Serves: 4 people

Prep Time: 15 minutes

Rest Time: 4-6 hours (soaking)

Cooking Time: 15-20 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper (optional)
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 green chilies (finely chopped)
  • 1 tsp ginger (grated)
  • Few curry leaves (chopped)
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions:

  1. Wash and soak urad dal for 4-6 hours.
  2. Drain the water and grind the dal to a smooth, thick batter using minimal water.
  3. Add salt, chopped onions, green chilies, ginger, curry leaves, and black pepper to the batter.
  4. Mix well and let the batter rest for 10-15 minutes.
  5. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
  6. Wet your hands, shape the batter into small doughnut-shaped vadas, and gently slide them into the hot oil.
  7. Fry until golden brown and crisp on both sides. Remove and drain excess oil on paper towels.
  8. Serve hot with coconut chutney or sambar.

FAQs:

Q: Can I make Medu Vada without onion?
A: Yes, you can omit onions. The vada will still taste delicious.

Q: How do I make soft inside vadas?
A: Grind urad dal to a smooth, fluffy batter and avoid adding too much water. Resting the batter helps too.

Q: Can I make vadas crispy?
A: Fry on medium heat and avoid overcrowding the pan. This ensures crispiness.

Q: Can I store batter?
A: Store in the fridge for up to 1 day. Mix gently before frying.
