Sarso Ka Saag

“Wholesome, Warm, and Wonderfully Green”

Serves: 3-4 people

Prep Time: 20 minutes

Rest Time: 5-10 minutes

Cooking Time: 60–70 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 500g mustard greens (sarson), cleaned and chopped
  • 100g spinach leaves (optional, for milder taste)
  • 2 medium onions (finely chopped)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (chopped)
  • 2-3 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 1-inch piece ginger (grated)
  • 2-3 green chilies (slit)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 2 tbsp maize flour (for thickening, optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2-3 tbsp ghee or oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves (chopped)

Instructions:

  1. Wash and chop mustard greens and spinach. Boil in water with a pinch of salt until soft, around 20–25 minutes. Drain and mash coarsely.
  2. Heat ghee or oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Sauté for a few seconds until aromatic.
  4. Add chopped onions and cook until golden brown.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft and pulpy.
  6. Add the mashed greens. Mix well, sprinkle turmeric and garam masala. Add maize flour if you want a thicker consistency.
  7. Simmer for 5-10 minutes. Adjust salt. Garnish with coriander leaves and a dollop of ghee if desired.
  8. Serve hot with makki ki roti (cornmeal flatbread) or steamed rice.

FAQs:

Q: Can I use only spinach instead of mustard greens?
A: You can, but the flavor will be milder and less pungent than traditional sarso saag.

Q: How do I make it creamier?
A: You can add a little cream or butter at the end, or cook the greens with some boiled potatoes for a smoother texture.

Q: Is Sarso Saag vegan?
A: Yes, if you use oil instead of ghee, it is completely vegan.

Q: How can I store leftovers?
A: Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 days. Reheat before serving.
