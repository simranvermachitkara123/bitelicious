Dahi Bhalla

"Soft, Spongy, and Soaked in Flavor—Dahi Bhalla Delight!"

Serves: 4-5 people

Prep Time: 30 minutes

Soaking Time: 2 hours

Chilling Time: 1 hour

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/4 cup moong dal (optional, for softness)
  • 2 cups thick curd (well beaten)
  • 1/2 tsp ginger paste
  • 2 green chilies (finely chopped)
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying
  • 1/2 tsp roasted cumin powder
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp black salt
  • 1 tbsp tamarind chutney
  • 1 tbsp green chutney (mint & coriander)
  • A pinch of chaat masala
  • Fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
  • Sev or pomegranate seeds for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Rinse urad dal (and moong dal if using) thoroughly. Soak in water for 4–6 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain and grind the dal into a smooth, fluffy batter. Add ginger paste, chopped green chilies, and salt. Mix well.
  3. Heat oil in a deep pan. Drop spoonfuls of batter into hot oil and fry until golden and crisp. Remove and drain excess oil.
  4. Soak the fried bhallas in warm water for 20–30 minutes. Gently squeeze out excess water without breaking them.
  5. Place the bhallas in a serving dish. Pour well-beaten curd over them generously.
  6. Drizzle tamarind and green chutneys. Sprinkle roasted cumin powder, red chili powder, black salt, and chaat masala.
  7. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves, sev, or pomegranate seeds if desired.
  8. Chill for 1 hour before serving for best flavor. Serve cold and enjoy!

FAQs:

Q: Can I use moong dal instead of urad dal?
A: Yes, moong dal can be used for a softer texture, or mixed with urad dal for balance.

Q: Can I make Dahi Bhalla without frying?
A: You can try air frying or baking, though the texture will differ from traditional deep-fried bhallas.

Q: Is Dahi Bhalla vegan?
A: Not typically, as it uses dairy curd. You can use plant-based yogurt for a vegan version.

Q: How do I store leftovers?
A: Store assembled bhallas in the fridge for up to 2 days. Keep chutneys and curd separate if storing longer.

Q: Can I prepare bhallas in advance?
A: Yes! Fry and soak them ahead of time. Refrigerate and assemble with curd and toppings just before serving.
