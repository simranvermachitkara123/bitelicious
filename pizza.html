Margherita Pizza

“Crispy crust, gooey cheese, fresh basil — Italian perfection.”

Serves: 2–3 people

Prep Time: 25 minutes

Rest Time: 1 hour (dough)

Cooking Time: 12–15 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp instant yeast
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 3/4 cup warm water
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 cup pizza sauce (homemade or store-bought)
  • 150 g fresh mozzarella cheese (sliced)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • 1 tbsp extra virgin olive oil (for topping)

Instructions:

  1. Mix flour, yeast, sugar, and salt in a bowl. Add warm water and olive oil, knead into a soft dough.
  2. Cover and let the dough rest for about 1 hour until it doubles in size.
  3. Preheat the oven to 220°C (430°F). Place a baking tray or pizza stone inside.
  4. Roll out the dough into a thin circle. Place on parchment paper.
  5. Spread pizza sauce evenly over the base.
  6. Top with sliced mozzarella and fresh basil leaves.
  7. Drizzle a little olive oil on top.
  8. Bake for 12–15 minutes until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly.
  9. Slice and serve hot!

FAQs:

Q: Can I use regular cheese instead of mozzarella?
A: Yes, but fresh mozzarella gives the authentic Margherita flavor.

Q: Do I need a pizza stone?
A: Not mandatory. A baking tray works fine, just make sure it’s preheated.

Q: Can I make the dough ahead?
A: Yes, store it in the fridge for up to 24 hours. Let it come to room temperature before baking.

Q: How do I store leftovers?
A: Keep slices in an airtight container in the fridge for 2–3 days. Reheat before serving.
