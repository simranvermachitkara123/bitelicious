Chole Bhature

“Spicy Chickpeas. Fluffy Bhature. Pure North Indian Delight.”

Serves: 3-4 people

Prep Time: 20 minutes + soaking

Rest Time: 8 hours (for dough)

Cooking Time: 40 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (chole)
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 large onion (chopped)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (chopped)
  • 2 green chilies (chopped)
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • Fresh coriander leaves (chopped)
  • For Bhature:
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp yogurt
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • Water (for kneading)
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions:

  1. Soak chickpeas overnight. Drain and pressure cook with water and salt until soft.
  2. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and sauté until aromatic.
  3. Add onions and cook until golden. Then add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies.
  4. Add tomatoes, coriander powder, turmeric, red chili powder, and garam masala. Cook until tomatoes soften.
  5. Add cooked chickpeas with some water and simmer for 10–15 minutes. Garnish with coriander leaves.
  6. For Bhature: Mix flour, baking powder, salt, and yogurt. Knead into a soft dough, adding water as needed. Rest for 8 hours.
  7. Divide dough into balls, roll into circles, and deep fry in hot oil until golden and puffed.
  8. Serve hot chole with fluffy bhature and onion slices.

FAQs:

Q: Can I use canned chickpeas?
A: Yes, reduce cooking time since canned chickpeas are already soft.

Q: Can I bake bhature instead of deep frying?
A: Baking is possible but bhature won’t puff fully and will be less soft.

Q: Can I make chole vegan?
A: Yes, this recipe is naturally vegan if you avoid ghee.

Q: How can I store leftovers?
A: Store chole in the fridge for 2–3 days. Bhature is best eaten fresh.
