Samosa

“Crispy, Spiced, and Perfectly Golden – That’s the Samosa.”

Serves: 4-5 people

Prep Time: 30 minutes

Rest Time: 20 minutes

Cooking Time: 25 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • Water (as needed for dough)
  • 2 medium potatoes (boiled and mashed)
  • 1/2 cup peas
  • 1 small onion (chopped)
  • 2 green chilies (chopped)
  • 1 tsp ginger (grated)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for deep frying
  • Fresh coriander leaves (chopped)

Instructions:

  1. Mix flour, salt, and oil in a bowl. Add water gradually and knead into a firm dough. Rest for 20 minutes.
  2. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and sauté briefly.
  3. Add onions, green chilies, and ginger. Cook until onions turn soft.
  4. Add mashed potatoes, peas, coriander powder, turmeric, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for 3-4 minutes.
  5. Divide dough into small balls. Roll each into a circle and cut into halves.
  6. Form cones with the dough halves and fill with the potato mixture. Seal edges using water.
  7. Heat oil in a deep pan. Fry the samosas until golden and crisp.
  8. Serve hot with chutney or ketchup.

FAQs:

Q: Can I bake samosas instead of frying?
A: Yes, brush with oil and bake at 200°C for 25-30 minutes until golden.

Q: Can I make samosas in advance?
A: You can prepare and freeze them uncooked. Fry or bake directly from frozen.

Q: Are samosas vegan?
A: Yes, if you use oil instead of ghee and avoid any dairy-based fillings.

Q: How can I store leftovers?
A: Keep in an airtight container in the fridge for 1-2 days. Reheat in an oven or air fryer for crispiness.
